Assessments

Language: All assessments can be in German or English or mixed.

Assessments are conducted in accordance with the module description.

  1. Intermediate presentation (30%)
    1. Find a dataset, use descriptive analysis (e.g. show the histogram of the values, show some summary statistics, how many data points)
    2. Find one question that you want to answer based on the data
    3. Apply one method (e.g. k-means, random forest, logistic regression) to your dataset
    4. Answer your question
    5. Analyze your answer, your model, the results
    6. Presentation: max 10 minutes, 5 minutes Q&A
      1. Criteria: All correct, can explain the method and the results
    7. Show precisely how your presentation relates to 2 - 4 learning goals of our course.
    8. Short report (about 2-4 pages). Criteria: Describe your dataset and the question you want to answer. Explain the method, your results and do a critical evaluation of the methods and your results 
    9. Hand in your presentation and your short report on the same day
  2. Final presentation (70%)
    1. Find a dataset, use descriptive analysis (e.g. show the histogram of the values, show some summary statistics, how many data points)
    2. Find four questions that you want to answer based on the data
    3. Apply four methods (e.g. k-means, random forest, generative AI, neural networks) to your dataset
    4. Answer your questions
    5. Analyze your answers, your models, the results
    6. Presentation: max 15 minutes, 5 minutes Q&A
      1. Criteria: All correct, can explain the methods and the results
    7. Short report (about 8 pages). Criteria: Describe your dataset and the question you want to answer. Explain the methods, your results and do a critical evaluation of the methods and your results 
    8. Hand in your presentation and your short report on the same day

Project Work

As part of the Machine Learning and Innovation Design module, students work in teams on a practice-oriented project in which machine learning methods are systematically applied to solve an innovative, data-driven design problem. The task involves identifying a concrete AI-suitable challenge, preparing relevant data sources, selecting and evaluating appropriate ML models, and implementing a functional prototype in Python.

The project covers all phases of an innovation process: idea generation, evaluation, prototypical implementation, and validation. The focus is on developing an application-oriented, human-centered solution with a clear value proposition, while also considering ethical and entrepreneurial implications.

In addition, students are required to submit a written report documenting the project workflow, the methods used, and a critical reflection on the results. The project work is a central component of the overall module grade.
